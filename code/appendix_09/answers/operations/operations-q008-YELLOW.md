## Analysis Highlights
**Strengths:**
- Prioritization of safety stock levels based on a conservative inventory approach, mitigating the risk of stockouts while maintaining a reasonable level of inventory.
- Recommendation of cycle counting and regular inventory audits to proactively address obsolescence, reducing the likelihood of significant losses.
- Incorporation of basic reorder point calculations to optimize safety stock levels, ensuring adequate supply without excessive holding costs.
- Focus on a combined approach – minimizing working capital needs and minimizing obsolescence – a strategic imperative for long-term operational stability.

**Weaknesses:**
- Lack of specific data on current inventory turnover rates and historical obsolescence rates to inform safety stock levels. Quantitative benchmarks are needed for a more precise assessment.
- The proposed policies are relatively broad. More detailed analysis, including cost-benefit calculations for each policy option, is required to fully determine their optimal implementation.
- No consideration of supplier lead times or potential for price fluctuations impacting obsolescence risk. Additional risk mitigation strategies should be considered.
- No assessment of different obsolescence risk profiles across product categories to tailor policies appropriately. A segmentation approach would be beneficial.
